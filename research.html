Raising the Bar-ometer: Identifying a User's Stair and Lift Usage

Ravikiran Arasur iWOAR 2024 Published in Springer Human Activity Recognition

We present a fully independent, real-time pipeline for multi-modal Human Activity Recognition specifically distinguishing between stair and elevator usage using wearable acceleration and barometric pressure sensors. The system demonstrates the underexplored utility of pressure data in HAR pipelines and achieves robust activity discrimination with classical ML methods.

Novel architecture design for multi-modal gesture recognition University of Siegen, 2025.

Thesis Title

Multi-Modal Hand Pose Estimation System Using Sensor Fusion and Deep Learning

The central contribution of this thesis is a novel DeepConvLSTM architecture with a dedicated multimodal fusion layer, designed to process simultaneous inputs from an IMU-equipped sensor glove and a video stream learning spatial and temporal representations jointly from two fundamentally different data modalities.

The system performs multi-class gesture classification, precision finger tracking, and full hand pose reconstruction in real time. The architecture was designed with strict attention to software extensibility enabling clean integration of additional sensor modalities as future work.

Built entirely in PyTorch with OpenCV and MediaPipe for the vision pipeline. The codebase is structured as a modular research framework, not a single-task model.

Architectural Components
DeepConvLSTM
Stacked convolutional layers feeding into LSTM cells captures local temporal patterns before modeling long-range dependencies.
Multimodal Fusion Layer
Novel dedicated layer combining IMU stream embeddings with video-derived skeleton features at a learned representation level.
IMU Sensor Pipeline
Raw acceleration, gyroscope, and magnetometer data from a wearable glove preprocessed and windowed for temporal modelling.
Vision Pipeline
MediaPipe hand landmark extraction feeding normalized 3D joint coordinates into the fusion architecture.
